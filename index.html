Temple History

The Icchapurti Hanuman Mandir is believed to be over 500 years old. Local legends say that Lord Hanuman himself blessed this site, granting peace, protection, and wish-fulfillment to devotees. For centuries, villagers and pilgrims have prayed here and experienced divine blessings. The temple walls have seen generations of faith, devotion, and seva. Today, the structure requires urgent renovation to safeguard this sacred space and continue the spiritual tradition for many more centuries.

ЁЯЩП Icchapurti Hanuman Temple Story ЁЯЩП This Hanumanji has been located on Indrayani Fort since 1831. Around 1978, Hanumanji appeared in the dream of a farmer from IndraiWadi village and told him: "I am situated on Indrayani fort at a height of 1300 feet, build my temple." Then, with some village friends, Shri Kashinath Jadhav and Bhavrao Jadhav brought Hanumanji's idol from the fort to IndraiWadi village and placed it under a tree. In 1998, temple construction work started, but due to lack of funds, it remained incomplete. Now in 2025, the temple has gone into a fully dilapidated state: - Water leaks from the roof - Hanumanji's idol has worn out - Lord Ganesha's idol is damaged - Nandi Maharaj's legs are broken - The walls of the temple are collapsing Therefore, our team "Icchapurti Hanuman Foundation" has taken up the reconstruction of this temple.
